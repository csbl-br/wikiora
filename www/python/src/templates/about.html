About WikiORA

WikiORA integrates Wikidata, Gene Ontology, PanglaoDB, and Wikipedia for simple, encyclopedic enrichment analysis.

WikiORA is a tool designed to simplify the process of gene set over-representation analysis by integrating data from Wikidata and Wikipedia. Our aim is to provide an easy-to-use platform for researchers to identify significantly enriched gene sets in their data, using a combination of curated gene sets from various sources.

How It Works

WikiORA uses the following steps to perform over-representation analysis:

  1. A list of genes is provided by the user.
  2. The background gene sets are defined using data curated into Wikidata.
  3. For each gene set, the overlap between the user-provided gene list and the genes associated with the gene set (and its Wikipedia page) is calculated.
  4. The p-value is calculated using the hypergeometric test, representing the probability of observing at least as many overlapping genes by chance.
  5. The Benjamini-Hochberg correction is applied to account for multiple testing and adjust the p-values.
  6. The odds ratio (provided in the download file) is calculated as follows: oddsRatio = (1.0 * a * d) / Math.max(1.0 * b * c, 1).
  7. The combined score (provided in the download file) is calculated as follows: combinedScore = -log(p) * oddsRatio.
  8. Results are sorted by p-value to highlight the most significantly over-represented terms.
